# Introduction to Dinoco

This library was created with the aim of facilitating database management. Inspired by the philosophy of [Prisma](https://www.prisma.io/), Dinoco brings a fluid, typed, and extremely productive interface to the [Rust](https://www.rust-lang.org/) ecosystem.

---

## The Prisma Experience in Rust

Dinoco is born from the desire to bring Prisma's agility and strong typing to the [Rust](https://www.rust-lang.org/) ecosystem. We believe that you shouldn't have to spend hours configuring complex macros or writing manual SQL for common day-to-day operations.

## Project Pillars

- **DX (Developer Experience) first:** A fluid and intuitive API that reduces cognitive load and accelerates development.
- **End-to-end type safety:** We ensure your data is always in sync with your type definitions, catching errors at compile time.
- **Uncomplicated relationships:** Forget exhaustive manual joins. Define your relationships and let Dinoco handle the complexity behind the scenes.
- **Powerful CLI:** Integrated migration management and code generation to keep your workflow organized and fast.

## Where do we stand?

The [Rust](https://www.rust-lang.org/) ecosystem has powerful tools, but they often come with a steep learning curve. **Dinoco** emerges to fill the gap between current options:

- **Compared to [Diesel](https://diesel.rs/):** While Diesel requires deep SQL knowledge and has a complex DSL (Domain Specific Language), Dinoco prioritizes a clean, productivity-focused syntax, without you having to fight the compiler for simple queries.
- **Compared to [SeaORM](https://www.sea-ql.org/SeaORM/):** SeaORM is excellent, but its layer-based architecture can be excessively verbose for many projects. Dinoco removes unnecessary "boilerplate code," delivering the same robustness with far fewer lines of code.

If you're looking for [Rust](https://www.rust-lang.org/)'s performance with the fluidity that Prisma brought to the Node.js world, Dinoco is the right choice for your next project.
